Maomao:


Chair, 2016.

New York, New York, USA.

With: Lillian Li.

Photography: Keith Greenbaum Studio.


"Maomao is not a chair--at least not until it's sat on. It is made from three materials and one element: a steel frame, faux fur, and a rubber bladder filled with air. In its purest state, it is a simple geometric relationship: a sphere in a cube, or a circle in a square. The relationship of the materials becomes dyanmic when it's forced to negotiate with the body.

When the weight of a body pushes down on the sphere, the frame resists deflection and holds the sphere in place. As the sphere compresses under the weight of the sitter, the frame becomes an arm rest and the shpere, a seat.

Maomao is not a chair, at least not until it's been sat on."
