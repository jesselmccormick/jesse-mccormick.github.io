Aticama:


Courtyard House, In Progress: 2018--

Aticama, MX


"The house in Aticama is an exploration into designing across distance, and with limited means. This is a project for a family that has been visiting this beachside town for generations, to have a more permanent place to stay regularily and host friends and relatives. Built on the top of a hill overlooking the Pacific Ocean, the purpose of the project is still somewhat open-ended. The project operates under a few constraints; it needs to have views of the ocean from the roof/second floor, the mango tree in the center of the courtyard needs to be preserved, and construction needed to be under $10,000, at least for the first phase. The projects takes the scale of the CMU brick as the starting point to define the rest of the house.

The plan of the house is composed of 5 even squares, which centralize the vertical circulation and water and electricity. There is no completed drawings on purpose --the plan above shows one configuration, but the house is designed to evolve with time and use, to continue to grow with the family, and with new combinations of uses. Forever under construction."
