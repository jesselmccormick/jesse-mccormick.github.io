NYC APT:


Apartment and Performance Space, In Progress: 2020--

NY, NY. USA

With: IGG Office for Architecture,

Future Projects Architecture,

Zachary White.


"With an existing space of 80' x 25' we are tasked with the challenge of converting former artist's lofts into a residence that doubles as a performance space, without compromising the openness. Understanding both the day-to-day life for the client as he blurs the boundaries of what is and is not performance, what is and is not private, the design employs partitions; some fixed some moving, and never full walls or full 'rooms.'

We have also come to understand this project in particular as an exploration of architecture as non-static, and have invited the client to choreograph a series of performances (such as a 1:1 scale plan 'taping') throughout design and construction in to explore the theoretical framework that architecture is not and should not be considered static, but always in motion and always in flux.

By freeing itself of walls and rooms, the project is pushed to consider the measure of time and space as a permanent negotiation with the body."
