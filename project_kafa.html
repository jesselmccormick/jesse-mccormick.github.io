Kafa:


Book, 2016.

Istanbul, TR

With: Nadir Pucinelli.

Published by: Mas Matbaa, Istanbul.


Project Text by: Jesse McCormick and Nadir Pucinelli.


The Contents of this book represent a human head at a one-to-one scale. Through the superimposition of 1250 individual MRI images taken at an interval of 0.2mm, each page of the book contains a single layer of the scan in the order it was captured. The thickness of each sheet of paper corresponds to the section of the head represented by the image.

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) represents three-dimensional information by compiling a series of sectional scans. Imaging technology lacks a certain capacity to represent the the physicality of the object, as the display of these images is limited to the sectional view of a digital screen. By virture of the MRI format as an accumulation of layers. the form of the book gives dimension to this sequence of images.

The book then expresses the material quality of data through an analog interface. The book, through both form and content, represent the living makeup of the human head through a substitute material. As a simulacrum it provides a critical perspective of the construction and representation of the human being."
